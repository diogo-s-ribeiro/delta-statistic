Ancestral Character Estimation

 There are various approaches to ancestral character estimation, that include Maximum likelihood (ML), Bayesian inference, and parsimony-based methods (cannot be used to calculate delta). To perform ancestral character estimation, the tree is used as a framework for estimating ancestral character states at various nodes or branches with some degree of uncertainty. Maximum likelihood and Bayesian methods use statistical models to estimate the most probable character states for the ancestral nodes depending on observed trait data, the evolutionary model chosen, and the phylogenetic relationships among the organisms.

• MAP
 Calculates the marginal at each ancestral node and then chooses a state with the highest posterior probability for each, independently from each other.

• JOINT
 Calculates the joint combination of ancestral character states from across the tree that maximizes the likelihood of the data when analyzed together.

• MPPA
 The marginal posterior probabilities approximation, proposed by S. A. Ishikawa in 2019, for every node, it chooses a subset of likely states that minimizes the prediction error measured by the Brier score.
